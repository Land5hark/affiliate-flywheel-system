# Major Affiliate Networks Directory

Reference list of large, well-known affiliate networks worth checking for any product category before searching for individual manufacturer programs. This list covers network *categories* and how to search them — not live commission rates, which change and must be verified per-program at time of use.

## General/broad-catalog networks

- **Amazon Associates** — near-universal fallback for physical products. Low commission (~1-10% depending on category), 24-hour cookie. Good for trust/conversion, weak for margin. Almost always worth including as one of several programs, never the only one.
- **ShareASale** (now part of Awin) — large network, especially strong for home goods, outdoor, DIY, pet, and specialty consumer brands. Search their merchant directory by category.
- **CJ Affiliate (Commission Junction)** — skews toward larger/established brands, home improvement, tools, electronics.
- **Impact** — modern network, strong with DTC and subscription-based brands (good fit for recurring-commission searches).
- **Rakuten Advertising** — broad catalog, historically strong in retail and home goods.
- **Awin** — large in home/garden and DTC brands, especially outside the US.
- **ClickBank** — mostly digital products/info products; occasionally physical goods. Useful when a niche has an educational/course angle (e.g., "how to start container gardening" courses) alongside physical products.
- **PartnerStack** — SaaS and subscription software; relevant if a niche has a companion app/tool angle.

## Niche-specific / vertical networks

Every established hobby or trade niche tends to have at least one dedicated specialty network or marketplace with its own affiliate program, separate from the big generalist networks. (Example already found in this workspace: Drip Depot for irrigation.) Search pattern: "[niche] affiliate program" and "[niche] specialty retailer affiliate."

## Direct manufacturer programs

Many individual manufacturers run their own program (via Refersion, Awin, Impact, or a custom in-house system) rather than only listing through a big network. These often have better commission rates than the same product listed via Amazon, because there's no network cut. Always check the manufacturer's own site footer for "Affiliates," "Partners," or "Affiliate Program" links before assuming Amazon is the only option for that brand.

## Search patterns that surface programs fast

- `"[brand name] affiliate program"`
- `"[brand name]" site:shareasale.com OR site:cj.com OR site:impact.com OR site:awin.com`
- `"[niche]" affiliate program apply`
- Check the footer of any manufacturer's own website for "Affiliates" / "Partners" links
